01The farm

A working orchard where the harvest moved faster than the paperwork

The operation is a family-run citrus orchard in the NSW Riverina, the heart of Australian citrus country, running seasonal picking crews across multiple blocks and varieties through the winter harvest. Like most growers in the region, the systems holding it together were paper tally cards, a supervisor's memory and a whiteboard back at the shed.

Every bin picked generated information: who picked it, which block and row it came from, when it was filled and where it was left. Almost none of that survived the day. Tally cards were reconciled by hand after dark, bins sat in rows longer than anyone realised, and questions like "which crew is actually ahead" or "what did block 7 yield last week" could only be answered roughly, and only after the fact.

02The problem

Harvest decisions were being made on instinct, hours behind the orchard

Harvest is the highest-stakes window of the citrus year. Fruit quality decays with every hour a full bin sits in the sun, picker payments hinge on accurate tallies, and crew productivity varies enormously between workers and blocks. Yet the people managing it, the grower and their supervisors, were working from a picture that was at best a day old and at worst a guess.

The brief was not to bolt an enterprise farm-management suite onto a working orchard. Those tools assume office time nobody has and training nobody wants. The brief was a tool that lives where the work happens: something a supervisor can use one-handed in the row, that captures the harvest as it happens and hands the grower a live picture without adding a single administrative step.

03The approach

Built in the rows, not in a boardroom

Agri-Mate was designed by founders who have picked, supervised and managed citrus harvests themselves. Rather than starting from a feature list, the build started from the physical reality of a picking day: gloved hands, bright glare, dusty screens, patchy mobile coverage between rows, and workers for whom English is often a second language. Every screen was tested against one question: can this be done in seconds, standing next to a bin?

The data model mirrors how an orchard actually thinks: blocks, rows, varieties, crews and bins. Each bin scan links a picker, a location and a timestamp, so the same tap that credits a worker's tally also plots the bin on a map and feeds the day's yield picture. Nothing is entered twice, and nothing depends on someone remembering to write it down.

One principle runs through the product: capture without ceremony. If recording the harvest ever competes with doing the harvest, the harvest wins, so the recording had to become part of the motion itself.

Discovery in the orchard

Picking days shadowed end to end, tally card and payment workflows mapped, failure points identified from bin loss to disputed counts.

Field-first prototype

A deliberately minimal bin-tracking flow built and put in supervisors' hands mid-season, refined against real crews rather than assumptions.

Live deployment through harvest

Picker tallies, bin locations and block-level counts running in production across the season, with the paper system retired block by block.

04What was built

Agri-Mate: the harvest, visible in real time

In its current form, Agri-Mate tracks harvest worker output as it happens: who picked what, how much was picked, and where every bin sits in the orchard. The result is a shorter path from tree to truck, live visibility over workforce performance, and a tally that both grower and picker can trust.

Picker & bin tracking

Every bin is credited to the picker who filled it, building a live count by worker, team, block and day, and an evidence base both sides of a payment can agree on.

Bin location map

GPS and row-level positioning of every full bin, so tractor and forklift operators drive to the fruit rather than searching for it, shortening the time from picking to packing.

Supervisor dashboard

Real-time visibility of crew productivity across blocks, surfacing who is ahead, who needs support and where the day is actually up to, while it can still be acted on.

Field-to-shed link

The same data flows straight into Newterra's back office, so tallies, payroll inputs and yield records arrive reconciled instead of being rebuilt from paper each night.

05Under the hood

Simple in the field, structured underneath

Agri-Mate runs as a mobile-first React application on a Supabase (PostgreSQL) backend, with n8n handling the workflow automation that connects field data to Newterra's bookkeeping and payroll processes. Data is captured at block, variety and task level and lands in a standardised model, the same structure used across every Newterra client, which is what makes cross-farm and cross-season comparison possible later.

Because the app feeds Newterra's broader data engine, every bin scanned strengthens more than one orchard's picture. Aggregated and de-identified, the data underpins benchmarking, yield and cost insights, and future procurement and risk offerings for participating growers, while each individual farm's information remains its own.

06Where it stands

An honest roadmap, in the product and on this page

Agri-Mate is a working tool with a deliberate trajectory: from harvest tracker, to the core operating system for orchards and broadacre farms. This is what is running, what is being built, and what is planned.

Delivered

Real-time picker and bin tracking, live counts by worker, team, block and day, GPS/row-level bin location, and supervisor visibility across the harvest.

In build

Workforce layer: central worker register, onboarding workflows, digital sign-in and time tracking, and storage of Australian working-rights and compliance documents.

Planned

Machinery and asset tracking with equipment-hour logging and maintenance alerts, task scheduling from planting to harvest, sensor integrations, and cross-farm benchmarking feeding machine-learning-driven recommendations.
