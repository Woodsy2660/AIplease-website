01The practice

A busy specialist practice with data in all the right places, and none of the useful ones

Roy Cardiology is a single-specialist interventional cardiology practice led by A/Prof Andrew Roy, running consulting rooms in Sydney alongside hospital procedural sessions. Like most Australian specialist practices, it runs on Gentu, the cloud practice management platform from Magentus. Appointments, referrals, correspondence and billing all live inside it.

The information was there. Getting at it was the problem. Operational questions arrived at weekly practice meetings faster than the answers could be assembled: how many referrals came in this week and from whom, which are approaching expiry without a booking, what did non-attendance actually cost, which letters are still waiting on a signature. Each answer meant a separate manual export, a different date window, and a spreadsheet someone had to build by hand.

02The problem

Practice meetings ran on recollection rather than evidence

The weekly practice meeting is where a specialist practice actually gets managed. Scheduling gaps, referral follow-ups, outstanding accounts and correspondence backlogs all surface there. But without a shared, current picture of the practice, those meetings leaned on memory and instinct, and the same questions kept resurfacing week after week.

The brief was not another dashboard. Dashboards answer questions only when someone remembers to ask them. The brief was infrastructure that matches the way the practice already works: something that observes the practice continuously and arrives at the meeting already prepared.

03The approach

Prototype against real exports, design against a synthetic practice

The build started with the practice's own de-identified Gentu exports: invoice reports, item-level reports, banking reports, appointment lists and unresolved invoice ageing. Working from real report structures rather than idealised sample data surfaced the quirks early, including item columns that mix Medicare item numbers with internal billing labels, and exports whose date windows do not line up with one another. The data model was built to reconcile these sources against each other, with hard invariants confirmed against the source files.

Design ran in parallel using a fully synthetic practice, so no patient or practice data ever appeared in a design artefact. Three complete visual directions were built with identical data and compared side by side before one was selected and refined into the production interface: a calm, card-based system that presents each metric with its own date context rather than implying a single unified snapshot.

One editorial principle runs through every screen: observation, not judgement. The product notes what is worth noticing and offers options. It never tells a clinician or practice manager what they must do.

04What was built

Practice Insights: a rhythm layer, not a reporting tool

The centrepiece is Practice Insights, which turns continuous observation of the practice into structured documents timed to the practice's own cadence. Instead of asking anyone to go looking for answers, the answers arrive when the practice needs them: before the weekly meeting, at the end of the month, and immediately when something is time-sensitive.

Overview

A current picture of the practice across appointments, referrals, correspondence and accounts, with every figure presented alongside its own date context.

Action items

Things worth noticing, gathered in one place: referrals approaching expiry without a booking, letters awaiting signature, invoices ageing past their usual pattern.

Weekly and monthly reports

Structured documents generated on demand as downloadable exports, ready to anchor the practice meeting rather than replace it.

Notifications

A weekly update email, a briefing-ready notice ahead of meetings, and an urgent action digest when something cannot wait for the next cycle.

05Under the hood

Privacy at the boundary, built for the Gentu ecosystem

De-identification happens at the adapter boundary, before data reaches any analytical layer. Patient names, contact details and Medicare identifiers are stripped on ingestion; everything downstream works with internal identifiers and aggregate counts only. All infrastructure runs in AWS's Sydney region, keeping data residency entirely within Australia.

Today the platform ingests the practice's own Gentu report exports. The architecture was designed from the outset for the Gentu partner API, which is built on the FHIR R4 healthcare data standard: the current ingestion layer is deliberately isolated so that the move from file exports to a direct API connection is a swap, not a rebuild.
